# Presentation Practice

We found some cool patterns in Operation Data Storm. Now let's talk about them!

## Step 1: Pick a Graphic

Use a visualization from your project, or grab one of the sample graphs from a club officer.

A good graphic makes one clear point. Label your axes, give it a title, and keep it simple. If it feels cluttered, consider splitting it into two charts.

**One story per graphic is plenty.** If you have multiple graphs, ask yourself: do they all support the same big idea? If one feels like a side note, cut it.

## Step 2: Plan What to Say

Your graphic doesn't speak for itself — you do. Here's a simple structure to start with:

- **Where's the data from?** Give a quick sentence of context.
- **What am I looking at?** Walk through the axes, colors, or shapes.
- **What's the trend?** What pattern jumps out?
- **So what?** What would you tell a student or advisor based on this?

Focus on what you *found*, not how you found it. Aim for about a minute per graphic.

## Step 3: Present!

Come up front and share your graphic with the group. Expect 1–2 questions — just answer what you can. It's totally fine to say "I don't know."

This is low-stakes practice. The goal is to get comfortable talking about data. Have fun with it!
